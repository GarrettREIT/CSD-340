Medical

After my first job working for a before and after school program/all day summer daycare I completed my Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) course and exam and got a job at at non-profit, QLI (Quality Living Inc.) as a RT (Rehabilitation Trainer) where I did direct care for patients recovering from Traumatic Brain Injures (TBI), Spinal Cord Injuries (SCI), and Stokes. The photo is of me, another staff and a patient who had a stroke, I was the lead hunter for the company and would take high functioning patients out hunting as a part of our Life Path Program. As my interest in the medical field grew I obtained my Medication Aide License which allowed me to work closely with nursing to give patients medication. Nursing was top of mind and I was promoted to Medical Coordinator where I was the liaison/patient advocate for all doctor appointments. I spent all my time meeting with doctors and patients at various hospitals and specialty clinics. Before I left QLI I was spearheading a new for profit Tele program called TeleRehabilitation where we brought Psychology, Speech & Language Pathology, Physical & Occupational Therapy to patients throughout the nation via iPad and Tele Services.
